Cryptocurrency

Cryptocurrency is a form of alternative, digital currency that uses cryptographic security to develop a protected, safe economy. Cryptography is the process of using techniques to encrypt a message and give only those who have a secret key the ability to decipher it. Although there is currently no technique to develop unbreakable encryption, modern cryptography makes the information unprofitable to attack. Currently, the most popular form of cryptocurrency is Bitcoin (BTC) with an exchange rate of 1 BTC

Bitcoin ()

History

Wei Dai is credited with being the first to envision the implementation of distributed cryptocurrency in 1998. Later, in 2009, Satoshi Nakamoto, a pseudonymous developer, created the first form currency to not be influenced by an authoritative body or figure. Known as Bitcoin, this digital form of money drove the market for decentralized cryptocurrency to be prevalent in the modern, tech-based society.

About

Bitcoin is the most accepted and prominient form of cryptocurrency to date. It is also the first form of currency to not be controlled by a central authority (i.e. a government, a bank, etc.). Focused on making an economy driven by person to person transactions, bitcoin is cryptographically secure and is maintained by the users in its system.

The transactions carried out using Bitcoin are reliable and secure for the users. In a transaction, the Bitcoin(s) are moved from one user's address to the other user's address. To prevent user's from double spending their Bitcoins, every transaction is broadcast and put in the blockchain (list of past transactions). After a number of transactions are made, the previous transactions are timestamped and permanently embedded in the system's history.

Benefits

Bitcoin's decentralized economy has numerous, positive characteristics. The idea of having no central authority is beneficial when sending currency without needing to deal with a third party (i.e. a bank). As well as not having to carry out transactions with a third party, the supply and demand of Bitcoins are completely controlled by the network of users and software. This prevents the possible unethical or malicious manipulation of currency by a central authority.

Bitcoin transactions are very unconventional. Unlike the fiat currency used in most countries, these transactions were intended to be permanent/unchangeable. In addition, due to the absence of a third party handling the money, the transactions are significantly faster and cheaper to execute.

Criticism

Although Bitcoin may seem to possess several advantages, there are some drawbacks that investors should take into consideration. The most significant flaw in Bitcoin's economy is its instability. Due to the limited supply and growing demand, the value of Bitcoins (BTC) is constantly fluctuating. Hindering long term investors, Bitcoin's worth has more drastic and abrupt variation than traditional, centralized currency.

As well as the econonomy's high volatility, cryptocurrency is still a relatively new concept and is under development. Even in our modern society, Bitcoin and any other cryptocurrency is susceptible to attacks and subversion. Furthermore, there are many precarious factors that make Bitcoin unreliable. Bitcoin may currently be the leading form of deventralized cryptocurrency, but it is still possible that it can be deposed. Moreover, the government can potentially moderate Bitcoin's economy in the future. Bitcoin poses a threat to the stability of fiat currencies by making it difficult to collect data about the entire economic state of a country. This will instigate inconsistencies in exchange rates and inflation/deflation controlled by the central authority.
